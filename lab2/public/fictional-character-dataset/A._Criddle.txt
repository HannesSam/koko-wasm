A. Criddle was a reporter for the New York Chronicle in the 1920s. Criddle wrote an article, published in the 6 December 1926 issue of the newspaper, about how a recent spate of "mysterious occurrences" had caused thousands of dollars of property damage in New York, negatively impacting the city's shopkeepers, who were struggling to pay for repairs and lost goods.[1]
